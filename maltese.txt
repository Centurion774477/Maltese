Maltese is an opinionated package manager framework that keeps you in charge.
You provide Maltese with scripts for your dependencies, such as "brew install elixir", and Maltese packages them into a unified collection.

Maltese provides you with an idiomatic UI. You declare a key dependency; title it --
and then provide a couple setup scripts, or just one; up to you.

After you are done setting up your Maltese scripts, Maltese will compile itself into a native binary with all the scripts saved.
You just provide your users with the compiled binary install script, and then they get walked through every dependency for your project.

on the user side of things:
Maltese walks down the list of your dependencies and brings up your chosen install scripts.
The users can choose to let Maltese install the given dependency for them, or they can manually type/copy and paste the scripts into their terminal.
